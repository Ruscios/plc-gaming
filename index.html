PLCGAMING.COM  ·  Field build dossier

A real industrial controller, wired up to play video games.

One AutomationDirect Productivity1000 PLC — the kind of box that runs factory lines and water treatment plants — sits at the center of a booth where video games, a web SCADA dashboard, and hand-built control panels all speak the same language: Modbus TCP. Pull a physical throttle and an airship moves in Minecraft. Press a button and a rocket launches in Kerbal Space Program. First exhibited at Open Sauce 2026.

How it all connects

Every game and dashboard is a Modbus client. The PLC is the one shared server in the middle. Inputs flow in, the PLC runs the control logic, and outputs flow back out — each project on its own reserved coils and registers so nothing steps on anything else.

02 /

Two games, one controller

Both of these run end-to-end on the real hardware: a physical action on a panel becomes a Modbus write, the PLC's own ladder logic does the work, and the result shows up in the game.

Minecraft · Airship

Fly a Create-mod airship

A physical throttle, steering wheel, and lift lever drive a Create-mod airship in-game through a custom CC: Tweaked Modbus mod. Live altitude and velocity stream back out of the game to the PLC — a full closed loop.

Helm panel ▸ P1-622 ▸ CC:Tweaked ▸ Minecraft

Kerbal Space Program

Launch a rocket to orbit

Press a button on the console and the PLC flies a complete launch, ascent, and orbital-circularization sequence — up to a ~100 km orbit — through a kRPC↔Modbus bridge. Fully controller-driven, flown repeatably.
